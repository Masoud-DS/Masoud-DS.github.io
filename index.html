About

A decade at the meeting point of fuels, process design and economics.

I am a mechanical engineer (Ph.D., Tarbiat Modares University; research visit at INP Toulouse / CNRS) working on low-carbon fuels, techno-economic analysis, and process design. A decade of research at the Renewable Energies Research Institute and as Assistant Professor at the University of Tehran sits alongside five industry-funded projects worth roughly €95,000 — four of them led by techno-economic analysis.

Since 2025 I have been a Visiting Faculty member at Sultan Qaboos University in Muscat, working on green-hydrogen pathways, hybrid solar–wind systems, and the techno-economic and life-cycle modelling that connects them to real deployment in the Gulf.

Most of my day-to-day work is quantitative: analysing fuel-production and fuel-utilisation pathways, building Excel- and HOMER-based decision tools, drafting LCA and TEA case studies, and writing the engineering side of scale-up — from chemistry through capex to operating envelope.

Research Focus

Four threads, one question: what does the clean transition actually cost?

Each area below is backed by published work, industry projects, or manuscripts currently in the pipeline.

💧

Green Hydrogen Pathways

Production, storage and recovery of hydrogen; techno-economic assessment of hydrogen energy storage under high-solar conditions, and downdraft gasification for hydrogen-bearing syngas.

📊

Techno-Economic Analysis

Process-scale TEA from chemistry to capex: glycerin-route economics, bio-lubricant line scale-up, and comparative TEA of energy-storage technologies for hybrid systems.

♻️

Life-Cycle Assessment

ISO-14040 LCA and carbon-footprint accounting, including comparative LCA of reactor configurations and bio-lubricant production from waste cooking oil.

⚙️

Hybrid Renewables & Process Design

HOMER Pro modelling of solar–wind systems, Aspen HYSYS process simulation, reactor CFD, and process intensification through oscillatory, microwave and ultrasound reactors.
